A wind turbine is a device that converts the kinetic energy of wind into electrical energy. It has large blades that catch the wind and rotate, turning a shaft connected to a generator. The generator then produces electricity through electromagnetic induction, a principle discovered by Michael Faraday. Wind turbines are mounted on tall towers to capture stronger winds and are controlled automatically to operate efficiently. There are two main types: horizontal-axis wind turbines, which look like large fans, and vertical-axis wind turbines, which can capture wind from any direction. They are widely used in wind farms, remote areas, and offshore locations to provide clean and renewable energy
