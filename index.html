The Legacy of the Wind Wheel

For centuries, the Wind Wheel was a symbol of human ingenuity. Unlike modern electricity-generating turbines, ancient windmills used mechanical power directly to grind grain or pump water. These "wheels" relied on simple drag forces to turn massive wooden gears.

Modern Wind Physics

Today, wind energy is a form of solar energy. The uneven heating of the atmosphere creates wind, which turns turbine blades around a rotor. This rotor is connected to a main shaft, which spins a generator to create electricity.
